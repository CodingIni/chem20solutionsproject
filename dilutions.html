Preparing Solutions and Dilutions




Preparing Solution


Before starting anything, first we need to calculate the mass of solute needed using the formula below:

$$n = C \times V$$

$$m = n \times M$$

For example, if we want to prepare 1L of a 0.05mol/L solution of NaCl, we would calculate the mass of NaCl needed as follows:

\( m = (0.05 \, \text{mol/L} \times 1.0 \, \text{L}) \times 58.44 \, \text{g/mol} \)

\( m = 2.922 \, \text{g} \)

So, we would need to weigh out around 3.0 g of NaCl.

Equipment Needed:

  • Beaker
  • Stirring rod
  • Volumetric flask
  • Electronic scale
  • Small funnel
  • Distilled water

Steps to Prepare a Solution:

  1. Measure: Weigh out the required mass of solute. Make sure to measure it in a beaker first.

  2. Dissolve: Dissolve the solute in half the amount of distilled water required in the beaker.

  3. Transfer:Pour the solution to a volumetric flask of the appropriate volume. Make sure to rinse the beaker with distilled water and add the rinse to the volumetric flask.

  4. Fill:Fill the volumetric flask to the final volume marked on the side. Use a dropper to ensure that the bottom of the meniscus aligns with the volume mark on the flask accurately.

  5. Mix:Using a stopper, invert the flask several times to mix the solution uniformly.




Diluting a Solution


To dilute a concentrated standard solution, we use the dilution formula:

$$C_1V_1 = C_2V_2$$

For example: If you have a 2.0 mol/L stock solution and want to make 500 mL of a 0.5 mol/L solution:


\( V_1 = \frac{0.5 \, \text{mol/L} \times 0.5 \, \text{L}}{2.0 \, \text{mol/L}} = 0.125 \, \text{L} \)


\( V_1 = 125 \, \text{mL} \)


So, you would need 125 mL of the stock solution.

Additional Equipment Needed:

  • Volumetric Pipette (with pump)
  • Standard Solution
  • Volumetric Flask
  • Distilled Water

Steps to Dilute a Solution:

  1. Calculate: Use the dilution formula to find the volume of the solution needed.

  2. Transfer: Use a pipette to accurately measure out the solution and transfer it to a new volumetric flask.

  3. Fill: Add distilled water until the solution is a bit below the required mark.

  4. Final Touch: Use a dropper to bring the bottom of the meniscus exactly to the line.

  5. Mix: Stopper the flask and invert it several times to ensure the concentration is uniform.
