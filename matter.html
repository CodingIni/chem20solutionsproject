Matter

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. It is made up of tiny particles called atoms, which are the building blocks of all matter. Atoms can combine to form molecules, which are the smallest units of a compound that retain the properties of that compound.

Classification of Matter

Matter can be divided into two main categories: Pure Substances and Mixtures.

Pure Substances (Elements & Compounds)

Element: A substance consisting of only one type of atom (e.g., Oxygen (O2)), Gold (Au)).

Compound: Two or more different elements chemically bonded in a fixed ratio (e.g., Water (H2O), Salt (NaCl)).

Mixtures (Homogeneous & Heterogeneous)

Homogeneous (Solutions): Uniform composition throughout. You cannot see the individual parts (e.g., Apple juice).

Heterogeneous: Non-uniform composition; distinct phases are visible.

  • Suspension: Large particles that settle over time (e.g., Muddy water).
  • Colloid: Medium particles that don't settle and scatter light (e.g., Milk, Fog).
  • Emulsion: A mixture of two liquids that normally don't mix (e.g., Oil and Vinegar, Mayonnaise).

Ionic vs. Molecular Solutions

Property Ionic Solutions Molecular Solutions
Dissolving Process Dissociation: Seperates into its ions (e.g., NaCl = Na+ + Cl-). Dispersion: Whole molecules spread out (e.g., Sugar in water).
Conductivity Electrolyte: Conducts electricity due to free-moving ions. Non-electrolyte: Does not conduct electricity.
