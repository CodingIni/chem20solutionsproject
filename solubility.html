Solubility and Saturation




Factors Affecting Solubility


There are several factors that can affect the solubility of a substance, including:

  • Temperature:The solubility of solids increases with temperature, while the solubility of gases decreases with temperature.

  • Pressure: The solubility of gases increases with pressure, while the solubility of solids and liquids is unaffected by pressure.

  • Type of Solute and Solvent: The chemical properties of the solute and solvent can affect solubility. For example, polar solvents tend to dissolve polar solutes, while nonpolar solvents usually dissolve nonpolar solutes.

Saturated Solutions


A saturated solution is a chemical solution that cointains the maximum amount of solute that is able to be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature


Unsaturated Solutions


An unsaturated solution is a chemical solution that contains less solute than the maximum amount that can be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature.


Supersaturated Solutions


A supersaturated solution is a chemical solution that contains more solute than the maximum amount that can be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature.This typically occurs when a saturated solution is heated to dissolve more solute and then cooled down slowly, allowing the excess solute to remain dissolved temporarily. These solutions are unstable, and can begin to form crystals if disturbed.


Dynamic Equilibrium:

when the forward and reverse processes occur at the same rate, resulting in no observable change in the concentration of the solution. Once dynamic equilibrium is established, the concentration remains constant.
