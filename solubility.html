Factors Affecting Solubility

There are several factors that can affect the solubility of a substance, including:

  • Temperature: Generally, the solubility of solids increases with temperature, while the solubility of gases decreases with temperature.
  • Pressure: The solubility of gases increases with pressure, while the solubility of solids and liquids is generally unaffected by pressure.
  • Nature of Solute and Solvent: The chemical properties of the solute and solvent can affect solubility. For example, polar solvents tend to dissolve polar solutes, while nonpolar solvents tend to dissolve nonpolar solutes.

Saturated Solutions

A saturated solution is a chemical solution that cointains the maximum amount of solute that is able to be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature

Unsaturated Solutionls

An unsaturated solution is a chemical solution that contains less solute than the maximum amount that can be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature.

Supersaturated Solutions

A supersaturated solution is a chemical solution that contains more solute than the maximum amount that can be dissolved by a solvent at a specific temperature.

Dynamic Equilibrum:

when the forward and reverse processes occur at the same rate, resulting in no observable change in the concentration of the solution. Once dynamic equilibrium is established, the concentration remains constant.
